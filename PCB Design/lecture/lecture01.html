Overview

Welcome to the intergrated circuits and electronics (ICE) field session. It is my hope that you find this course something like an apprenticeship of electrical design, fabrication and assembly. There will be lots of hands-on work of the type that you or technicians working with you, required to bring products to life.

You will do this by designing a PCB for a basic linear power supply.

Materials needed for the class

The Colorado School of Mines will be paying for the electrical components needed for your power supply and 556 timer circuit. You however, are required to acquire some materials on your own to complete the course work. These include the following items.
  • 12V center-tap positive AC/DC wall transformer,
  • USB thumb drive ,
  • Bread board,
  • Needle nose pliers, wire stripper.

Weekly course structure

If you have taken Embedded Systems previously, then the lab stucture will be familar to you. We will start every week's class with a brief lecture of what is happening that week and how it fits into the overall structure of the class. You will then break into teams of 2 and proceed to start work on the inLab. The inLab is designed to lead you on an investigation of the concepts that you will need to complete that week's assignment. The inLab will usually have some questions that you need to answer and turn in by noon on the day following class. During the class session you may be asked to demonstrate your lab assignment from the previous week.

Lab 1

I like to think of schematics as the language of electrical engineering. They allow an EE to quickly describe the interconnection of circuit elements to perform some task. Consequently, the first part of today's lab asks you to look over an external document on schematics. Even if you are a crusty old EE with decades of experience, please take a few moments to quickly work through the tutorial. It's pretty well oprganized.

A search of "resistors" on DigiKey.com yields almost half a million different resistors that you can purchase. The reason for this diveresity is the wide variety of roles that resistors need to serve. The second section will introduce some of the key parameters for resistors, potentiometers and capacitors that you will be working with this term.

Finally you will examine the humble RC series circuit and deep dive in the charge and discharge behavior. This will be the launching point for this week's lab work.
