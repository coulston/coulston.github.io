Overview

You have completed a simple layout of the components in your 556 timer. A layout is never "correct" or "perfect". While an electrically correct layout is manditory, there is a lot of room for personal aesthetics to bias an evaluation of a layout. These subjective bias may come from personal experience or lessons passed down from others. So with experience, you will learn that you have to bend the rules sometimes to benifit the greater good.

Since we are building a power supply unit (PSU), you need to be comfortable with the theory of their operation and the practice of working with PSUs. The behavior of a PSU can be summarized with four variables:
Vset
The maximum voltage you want to see across the PSU outputs.
Vout
The voltage across the PSU outputs.
Iset
The maximum current you want to see throgh the PSU outputs.
Iout
The current being delivered through the PSU outputs.
