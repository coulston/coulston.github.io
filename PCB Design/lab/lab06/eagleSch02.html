Add components

Today we will focus on adding the constant current supply that you prototyped last week. We will start by adding the MCP6004, a quad operational amplifier to the schematic. When the term "quad" is used to describe an operational amplifier, it means that there are four operational amplifiers inside the single integrated circuit package. EAGLE can treat each one of the four operation amplfiers like its own component on the schematic even through they all belong on a single integrated circuit. To see what I mean do the following:
  • Select the ADD tool from the left toolbar
  • Search for *MCP*, go to the "linear" library, open the "MCP604" folder, and select the "MCP604SL" in the "SO14" package,
  • Your pointer will change to a operational amplifier symbol,
  • Left mouse click somewhere on the schematic,
  • Add five more operational amplifiers.
You should end up with something that looks like the following.


Notice that the first four operational amplifiers that you placed all had names "IC1*" where * was A-D. The letter designates which operational amplifier on the chip (shown at right) is being used. Only the "A" operational amplifier has the power and ground connections. Be careful when moving this particular operational amplfier as you can easily seperate the power pins from the operational amplfier. You should verify that the pin numbers on the schemtic agree with the pin out of the actual device.
